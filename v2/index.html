Kaiqiang Xu 徐凯强

My work seeks the convergence of technology and creativity. During my PhD at HKUST, I design efficient and accessible systems for machine learning, with publications in top conferences. Earlier, as COO at Vincross Robotics (2016-2020), I led a global team to make HEXA a world-recognized robot, and was interviewed by WSJ and IEEE.
Outside of work, I enjoy art and music whenever I can. I dance Argentine Tango and have a love for Golden Age Tango Music. My favorite artist is Edward Hopper for his Nightawks (1942) and more. I recommend the timeless musical Les Misérables to everyone. Besides my hometown Jinan, I called Ann Arbor, Beijing, Berkeley and Hong Kong home.

Research

My work focuses on making machine learning systems more efficient and accessible by optimizing parallelization, pipelining, and resource scheduling, driven by workload patterns in large-scale GPU clusters. These optimizations span networked systems, architecture, and data management.
